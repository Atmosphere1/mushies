Tomatoes

Let me take a guess... You have already heard about the legendary word "tomato", sometimes in its plural form, which is "tomatoes". Tomato is a famous fruit, with glossy red skin, occasionally yellow or purple covering the watery, pulpy flesh. It originated from Central and South America. The fruit's scientific name is "Solanum lycopersicum", which isn't as popular as its appealing, short name "tomato". Tomatoes are widely consumed by people all over the world. They also vary in size, color, shape and taste. Today, we're going to discover some of the most well-known tomato varieties. Let's hop right in!

Early Girl

One of the most popular varieties of tomatoes. It originated in France as a hybrid tomato, distributed in the U.S. in the late 1970s by PetoSeed company. The ripe fruit is red, about the size of a tennis ball, weighing about 110 - 230g. Early Girl tomatoes taste like tomatoes (obviously) with the acidity and sweetness balanced, sometimes a hint of tang.

Recommended dishes to make with Early Girl tomatoes: Sandwich, bagel or salad topping, tomato soup, sauce, jam,...

Beefsteak

Beefsteak, also known as Beef, is a variety of tomatoes that are very well-known amongst home growers and probably everyone. It was first brought to Europe by conquistador Hernan Cortez from Mexico in the early 16th century. The fruit is red, sometimes pink, can expand up to about 15cm in diameter, weighing about 453g. Beefsteak tomatoes still taste like tomatoes (because why not) with a firm, juicy flesh and thin, soft skin making them easier to slice.

Recommended dishes to make with Beefsteak tomatoes: Base for soup, sauce, dips, salad topping, stuffed,...
